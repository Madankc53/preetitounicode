Tihar 2083 Date — तिहार २०८३ | All 5 Days, Bhai Tika & Laxmi Puja Complete Guide

Tihar 2083 BS (Nepal's Festival of Lights) runs from Kartik 21 to Kartik 25, 2083 BS — November 7 to November 11, 2026 AD. The festival spans 5 days, each dedicated to worshipping a different deity or animal.

Laxmi Puja (Deepawali) — the main night of lights — falls on Monday, November 9, 2026 (Kartik 23, 2083). Bhai Tika — the final and most celebrated day — falls on Wednesday, November 11, 2026 (Kartik 25, 2083). The official Tihar government holiday period is Kartik 22–26 (November 8–12, 2026).

Kaag Tihar — Crow Worship
Saturday, November 7, 2026 Kartik 21, 2083 BS
Crows are worshipped as messengers of Yama (god of death). Food offerings — rice, bread, sweets — are placed on rooftops before sunrise for crows to eat. The day begins the Yama Panchak (five days of Yama worship).

Kukur Tihar — Dog Worship
Sunday, November 8, 2026 Kartik 22, 2083 BS
Dogs are garlanded with marigold flowers, given tika on their foreheads and offered special food including sel roti, meat and sweets. Dogs are revered as loyal guardians and companions of Yama. Even street dogs receive garlands and food on this day.

Day 3 ⭐ Main Night
लक्ष्मी पूजा / गाई तिहार
Laxmi Puja / Gai Tihar — Festival of Lights
Monday, November 9, 2026 Kartik 23, 2083 BS
The most spectacular night of Tihar. Cows (sacred to Hindus) are worshipped in the morning. In the evening, every home, shop and street is lit with hundreds of oil lamps, candles and LED lights to welcome Goddess Laxmi (goddess of wealth). Rangoli (colourful floor art) is drawn at entrances. Deusi-Bhailo singing groups go from house to house. Fireworks light the night sky across Nepal.
Main Day — National Holiday
🐂
Day 4
गोरु तिहार / म्ह पूजा
Goru Tihar / Mha Puja / Govardhan Puja
Tuesday, November 10, 2026 Kartik 24, 2083 BS
Oxen (Goru) are worshipped for their role in farming and agriculture. Among the Newar community, this day is celebrated as Mha Puja — a self-worship ceremony that also marks the Newar New Year (Nepal Sambat). Each person creates an elaborate mandala on the floor and worships their own soul. Govardhan Puja (worship of the hill Lord Krishna lifted) is also observed.
Newar New Year — Mha Puja
⭐
Day 5 ⭐ Bhai Tika
भाइ टीका
Bhai Tika — Sister-Brother Bond
Wednesday, November 11, 2026 Kartik 25, 2083 BS
The final and most celebrated day. Sisters apply a magnificent seven-coloured tika (made from oil, yogurt and coloured powders in 7 bands) on their brothers' foreheads, along with Jamara (barley seedlings from Dashain). Brothers offer sweets, money and gifts to sisters. The tika is believed to protect brothers from Yama (death) for the year. Brothers who cannot be present in person receive tika from aunts or cousins.
Main Day — National Holiday

🙏 Tihar Rituals — How Each Day is Celebrated

🕯️
Oil Lamp Lighting — Deepawali Tradition
Every home lights hundreds of oil lamps (diyo), candles and coloured lights on the night of Laxmi Puja. Lamps are placed on window sills, doorsteps, rooftops and along pathways. The entire country glows from above. The light is meant to guide Goddess Laxmi into the home and invite prosperity for the coming year.
🎨
Rangoli / Mandala Drawing
Intricate geometric or floral patterns called Rangoli (or Mandala for Newars) are drawn at the entrance of homes using coloured powders, flowers and rice. These are believed to welcome Goddess Laxmi and protect the household. In Newar culture, the Mha Puja mandala is an elaborate personal altar representing the self.
🎵
Deusi-Bhailo — Singing Groups
Groups of young people go from house to house singing traditional Deusi (for boys) and Bhailo (for girls) songs on the nights of Laxmi Puja and Bhai Tika. Homeowners give cash, sel roti (rice bread) and sweets. The songs praise the gods, bless the household and celebrate the season. The money collected often goes to community development.
💫
Bhai Tika Seven-Colour Tika
The Bhai Tika is unique — a seven-band tika made from seven different coloured materials: oil (black), yogurt (white), red powder (red), turmeric (yellow), blue, green and orange. Sisters apply each colour in a vertical stripe on their brothers' foreheads while chanting prayers to Yama to spare their brothers. Brothers give gifts, money and sweets in return. The ceremony can last several hours as extended family gathers.

📖 History & Significance of Tihar

तिहारको महत्त्व — Why Tihar is Nepal's Festival of Lights

Tihar (also called Deepawali or Yamapanchak) is Nepal's second-greatest festival after Dashain. The five-day celebration honours the relationship between humans and the natural world — crows, dogs, cows and oxen all receive worship before humans celebrate Bhai Tika. This reflects Nepal's deep-rooted belief that all living beings deserve respect and reverence.

The mythological basis of Tihar centres on Yama — the god of death — and his sister Yamuna. According to legend, Yamuna refused to let Yama come until she had applied tika to him, thus delaying death itself. This story is the foundation of Bhai Tika, where sisters symbolically protect their brothers from death for another year.

Laxmi Puja (the main night of lights) invites Goddess Laxmi — the deity of wealth, prosperity and fortune — into homes. The lights are meant to dispel darkness and ignorance, and the lit lamps guide the goddess to those who have prepared their homes with devotion and cleanliness.

The Newar community's Mha Puja adds a unique philosophical dimension — worshipping oneself as a divine being, recognising the soul's worth. This makes Tihar one of the few festivals worldwide that includes a formal celebration of self-worth and self-respect.
