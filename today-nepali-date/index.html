❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Today's Nepali date in Bikram Sambat is shown live at the top of this page. It is calculated using Nepal Standard Time (NST, UTC+5:45) and a verified month-length lookup table from the Nepal Panchanga Nirnayak Samiti (NPNS). The date updates automatically at midnight NST.
"Aaja ko miti" (आजको मिति) means "today's date" in Nepali. It refers to the current date in the Bikram Sambat (BS) lunisolar calendar — Nepal's official calendar used in government documents, banks, legal forms and daily life. Every Nepali school, office and court uses the BS date.
The Bikram Sambat (BS) calendar is approximately 56 years and 8 months ahead of the Gregorian (AD) calendar. For example, April 14, 2026 AD = Baisakh 1, 2083 BS. To convert: add 56 or 57 to the AD year (depending on whether you are before or after mid-April).
NST (Nepal Standard Time) is UTC+5:45 — unique in the world for its 45-minute offset. Nepal is 5 hours 45 minutes ahead of UTC/GMT, 15 minutes ahead of India (IST, UTC+5:30), and 10 hours 45 minutes ahead of New York (EST). Nepal uses a single time zone across the entire country.
Nepal uses the Bikram Sambat (BS) lunisolar calendar as its official national calendar. It accounts for both solar and lunar cycles, which is essential for calculating religious festival dates. The BS calendar was established around 57 BCE. It is used in all government documents, bank records, land certificates, legal proceedings and school records in Nepal.
