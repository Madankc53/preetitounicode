How Old Is Bikram Sambat? The Age of Nepal's Official Calendar

Calculating the ancient origins of the calendar that still governs Nepali life

2,082+ Years
Bikram Sambat was established in 57 BCE

As of 2025 AD (2081–2082 BS), the calendar is over 2,082 years old and counting.

Bikram Sambat (Vikram Samvat) is one of the oldest continuously used calendars in the world. According to popular tradition and historical records, it was founded in 57 BCE by Emperor Vikramaditya of Ujjain to celebrate his victory over the Sakas. This means the calendar has been in use for more than two millennia, making it significantly older than the Gregorian calendar (adopted in 1582 CE) and even older than the Common Era (CE/AD) system.

📐 How Is the Age of Bikram Sambat Calculated?

The age is determined by subtracting the calendar's epoch year from the current Gregorian year, accounting for the fact that there is no "year zero."

  • Epoch start: 57 BCE (also written as 57 BC)
  • Current AD year: 2025 (for this calculation)
  • Calculation: 57 + 2025 = 2,082 years

Why add 57 and 2025? Because the timeline from 57 BCE to 1 BCE is 57 years, and from 1 CE to 2025 CE is 2,025 years — total of 2,082 years. (There is no year 0 between BCE and CE.)

📌 Note: The exact age can vary slightly depending on the month. Since Bikram Sambat starts in mid-April (Baishakh), the calendar "turns" a year older each April. For example, in April 2025, Bikram Sambat will enter year 2082 BS — marking another milestone.

2025 CE (2082 BS)
Bikram Sambat reaches 2,082 years of age — still the primary calendar for all official, legal, and cultural purposes in Nepal.

🔢 Why Is Bikram Sambat 56.7 Years Ahead of AD?

The common confusion: if BS started in 57 BCE, why is the difference not exactly 57 years? Because the Bikram Sambat calendar's new year (Baishakh 1) falls in mid-April, while the Gregorian new year is January 1. Between January and April, the BS year is actually 56 years ahead, and after April it becomes 57 years ahead. On average, the offset is 56.7 years.

Example: On January 1, 2025 AD, the BS year is still 2081 (difference of 56 years). On April 14, 2025 AD, BS becomes 2082 (difference of 57 years). So the calendar's age is always "one year older" after Baishakh 1.

🌟 Why Does the Age of Bikram Sambat Matter?

A calendar that has survived for over 2,000 years carries immense cultural weight. For Nepal:

  • Sovereignty: Using an indigenous calendar distinguishes Nepal from colonially imposed systems.
  • Unbroken tradition: Festivals like Dashain and Tihar have been observed on the same BS dates for centuries.
  • Historical continuity: Ancient inscriptions and modern legal documents share the same calendar.
  • Identity: Bikram Sambat is as integral to Nepali identity as the flag or the language.
